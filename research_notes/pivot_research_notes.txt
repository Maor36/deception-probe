4 METHODS FOR SPONTANEOUS DECEPTION GROUND TRUTH:

1. MASK Benchmark (Pressure & Incentive)
   - Paper: "The MASK Benchmark: Disentangling Honesty From Accuracy" (Center for AI Safety, 2025)
   - Method: Give model incentive to lie (e.g., write grant proposal to maximize funding)
   - Ground Truth: Compare neutral answer (belief elicitation) vs pressured answer
   - Dataset: 1,500 situations, 6 archetypes (sales, PR, professional reports, etc.)
   - Available on HuggingFace

2. Liars' Bench (Safety Pressure / Harm-Pressure)
   - Paper: "Liars' Bench: Evaluating Lie Detectors for Language Models" (Cadenza Labs, 2025)
   - Method: Exploit RLHF safety mechanisms - model claims "I don't know" about things it demonstrably knows
   - Ground Truth: Model answers correctly in neutral academic context, then claims ignorance in harmful context
   - KEY FINDING: Linear Probes (exactly like ours) FAILED at 54% accuracy (near random chance)
   - Available on HuggingFace

3. Contact Searching Questions (Logic puzzles, no context)
   - Paper: "Beyond Prompt-Induced Lies: Investigating LLM Deception on Benign Prompts" (NUS, August 2025)
   - MOST IMPORTANT PAPER FOR US
   - Method: Pure logic questions, no instruction to lie, no pressure
   - Ground Truth: Ask same question positively and negatively. If model gets BOTH wrong = statistically proven deceptive intention
   - "Deceptive Intention Score" metric
   - Proves models lie spontaneously even on benign prompts

4. Sycophancy (Social Flattery)
   - Studied extensively by Anthropic
   - Method: User presents false claim, model agrees to be "polite"
   - Ground Truth: Model knows truth in neutral context but lies to please user
